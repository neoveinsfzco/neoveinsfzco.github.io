3: How do I search for variations on a phrase, like alternate spellings or the words within a specific proximity of each other?

Wildcard for Alternate Spellings


Use a wildcard (*) in a term to search for:

  • Alternate endings on terms within phrases (i.e., vaccin* for vaccine or vaccination)
  • Alternate spellings of terms within phrases (i.e., organi*ation for organization or organisation)

What does it mean to use a wildcard?

These are the key features of using a wildcard:

  • The wildcard is represented by an asterisk (*)
  • A wildcard can substitute for zero or more characters in a term or phrase
  • Terms must begin with at least four characters before the first wildcard (i.e., colo*)
  • You may use multiple wildcards in the same word (i.e., organi*ation*)
  • If using wildcards in a phrase, enclose the phrase in double quotes
  • Using a wildcard turns off Automatic Term Mapping (ATM)

Every term in a phrase can include a wildcard.

For example, to find variations on the phrase vaccine schedule, search for "vaccin* schedul*". This will search for phrases like:

  • vaccine schedule
  • vaccination schedule
  • vaccine scheduling
  • vaccination scheduling

Key Takeaway

Use a wildcard (*) in words to look for alternate endings on terms or alternate spellings of terms within a phrase. Enclose the phrase in double quotes.
